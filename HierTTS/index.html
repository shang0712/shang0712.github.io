HierTTS

HierTTS: Expressive End-to-End Text-to-Waveform using Multi-Scale Hierarchical Variational Auto-encoder

Under review

Abstract

Developing a high-quality and highly expressive Chinese speech synthesis system is very challenging. Since it is very difficult to generate speech waveforms directly from text, previous work has roughly split the text-to-waveform generation process into three stages: style extraction and prediction, acoustic modeling, and vocoder. Each stage is relatively independent, and the generation process from style to acoustic features to waveform is realized step by step. In this paper, we design a multi-scale end-to-end Chinese speech synthesis system based on the acoustic and linguistic properties of speech. The method employs a hierarchical variational self-encoder approach to model the unification of the three stages and models five scales of implicit variables at the frame level, phoneme level, subword level, word level, and sentence level. The encoder encodes the speech signal from fine-grained features to coarse-grained cryptovariables step by step, while each level of fine-grained features in the decoder relies on coarse-grained cryptovariables for generation. To prevent the posterior failure of the cascaded cryptovariables, we use stepped weights for the KL scatter at different scales, and smaller weights for the cryptovariables encoding long-time information.
