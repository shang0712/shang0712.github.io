Wavelet Analysis for Prominence in Bilingual End-to-End Speech Synthesis"

Authors:*
Abstract: When deploying bilingual text-to-speech (TTS) system, we focus on the consistency of prosody cross languages. However, the two mixed languages, Mandarin and English, belong to two language families that are phonetically and grammatically different. The lack of annotation and evaluation method in bilingual prosody limits the bilingual performance of the model. The difficulty is compounded by unbalanced training data. In this paper, we look into the problem with bilingual prominence. Specifically, we extracted the bilingual prominence with Continuous Wavelet Transform (CWT) and evaluate the consistency of bilingual prosody objectively. We evaluate our method on end-to-end model with prominence embedding. Our bilingual prosody is stable when only monolingual recordings of a source language speaker are available. Both similarity and naturalness of prosody make an improvement in our objective and subjective evaluation.

Monolingual dataset DataBaker( 12hours Mandarin female ) is used in the demo. The sample rate is 8KHz .

“This is your personal assistant, Google Home.”

Tacotron 2 learns stress and intonation.

(The speaker is instructed to stress on capitalized words in our training set. So simply capitalizing some words will change the overall intonation.)
“The buses aren't the problem, they actually provide a solution.”
“The buses aren't the PROBLEM, they actually provide a SOLUTION.”

Tacotron 2's prosody changes when turning a statement into a question.

“The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.”
“Does the quick brown fox jump over the lazy dog?”
