Abstract:

This paper proposes a multi-speaker multi-style multi-language speech synthesis scheme based on FastSpeech using monolingual datasets. It supports multi-language speech synthesis for each available timbre and allows the user to customize each language's speaking style. In the model, we carefully decouple the speech into three parts; dynamic style information, speaker-independent linguistics information, and static timbre information. In particular, we utilize a conditional variational encoder for timbre-free style extraction. Secondly, we also conducted adversarial speaker classifiers with gradient reversal layer on the representation of text and style encoder to avoid static timbre information leakage. Then, we built a style predictor for each extracted style. Style transfer could be accomplished by assign any style type at inference, which enables within-language and cross-language style transfer. The experimental results show that our method provides more style choice for multilingual speech synthesis and are more stable in sound quality.

 

 

Our training data includes six speakers, covering three languages. There are three speakers from mandarin (spk1, spk2, spk3), two from English (spk4, spk5), and one from German (spk6). Among them, spk4 speaks with an Indian accent. spk3 and spk5 are male speakers.
