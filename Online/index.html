Audio Samples from "Incorporating Stream Transformer for Stream Speech Synthesis"

Paper is under review.

Abstract:

In this paper, we unify streaming and non-streaming Text-to-Speech (TTS) and voice conversion (VC) in one model. As the two main tasks of speech synthesis, TTS and VC are widely used in our life. Non-streaming generation can greatly improve the speed of synthesis. However, in some real time scenarios, the ability to streaming generation is critical. For more flexibility, we have unified streaming and non-streaming generation into one model, with support for both TTS and VC. We developed over self-attention based parallel TTS framework. By introducing a Mel- spectrum encoder to realize the voice conversion, and by using the conditional GAN in training to improve the quality of synthesis. In order to unify streaming and non-streaming generation, we propose a chunk-based attention strategy to arbitrarily control the context length on the right, and set different chunks sizes for the encoder and decoder to match the characteristics of different time scales. At the inference, the inference latency can be easily controlled by changing the chunk size. Experiments show that unifying TTS and VC into one model does not harm the effect of either subtask. In addition, streaming test shows that streaming TTS are able to approach the non-streaming effect when the delay is two words. Streaming VC has almost no loss of naturalness in the case of 200ms delay.
