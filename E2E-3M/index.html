Abstract:

Benefits from the variational auto-encoder, truly end-to-end text-to-speech, integrating an acoustic model and a vocoder achieves higher sound quality and faster convergence. However, those end-to-end models are still problematic when extended to the multilingual setting due to unsuccessful decoupling of the timbre information. This work proposes a multilingual multi-speaker multi-style text-to-speech system by successfully disentangling the timbre, style, and content in an end-to-end manner, which improves end-to-end training based on VITS with two-level hierarchical VAEs. By combining conditional VAE into the hierarchical structure, our model can get speaker-independent multilingual pronunciation representations and phone-level style representations without the help of IPA or other disentangle techniques. Then we build a stochastic style predictor for each speaking style at the inference. Subjective human evaluations on naturalness and similarity (timbre, style) suggest that our proposed model achieves high quality comparable to natural sound at cross-language, cross-style, and code-switching synthesis.

 

We evaluated proposed system with our mandarin proprietary datasets, LJspeech and subset from css10. Languages such as Chinese, English, Spanish, German, and Uighur are covered. For simplicity, we only show results of three most representative languages including Chinese (CN), English (EN), German (DE).
