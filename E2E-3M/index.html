Abstract:

Although achieving high sound quality comparable to recording in single languages, recent fully end-to-end text-to-speech systems are not flexible enough to enable controllable speech synthesis. This paper presents an end-to-end multi-lingual multi-speaker multi-style text-to-speech system, which combines the advantages of both high quality and controllability. We treat end-to-end TTS as a hierarchical generation problem and unify acoustic model and vocoder into end-to-end training with a two-level Hierarchical VAE. Integrating Conditional VAE into the hierarchical structure, our system obtain cleaner representation by disentangling global timbre information successively at frame and phone levels. Besides, we encode fine-grained style at the phone level and build a style predictor for each speaker. Thus, diverse speech synthesis is accomplished by combining timbre and style from two different speakers within or cross languages. Subjective human evaluations on naturalness and similarity (timbre, style) suggest that our system achieves high MOS comparable to recording at multi-lingual and code-switching speech synthesis.

 

We evaluated proposed system with our mandarin proprietary datasets, LJspeech and subset from css10. Languages such as Chinese, English, Spanish, German, and Uighur are covered. For simplicity, we only show results of three most representative languages including Chinese (CN), English (EN), German (DE).
