Paper:

Paper is under review.

Abstract:

Although achieving high sound quality comparable to recording in single languages, recent fully end-to-end text-to-speech systems are not flexible enough to enable controllable speech synthesis and have a mediocre performance on prosody when synthesizing across languages. This paper presents an end-to-end multi-lingual multi-speaker multi-style text-to-speech system, which combines the advantages of both high quality and controllability. We treat end-to-end TTS as a hierarchical generation problem and innovatively use a two-level Hierarchical VAE to unify acoustic model and vocoder into parallel end-to-end training. Integrating Conditional VAE into the hierarchical structure, our system obtains cleaner representation by disentangling global timbre information successively at frame and phone levels. Besides, we overcome the unnatural prosody problem at cross-lingual speech synthesis by transferring existing styles in the target language. Subjective human evaluations on naturalness and similarity (timbre, style) suggest that our system not only enables diverse speech synthesis but achieves high MOS comparable to recording at multi-lingual and code-switching speech synthesis.

 

We evaluated proposed system with our mandarin proprietary datasets, LJspeech and subset from css10. Languages such as Chinese (CN), English (EN), and German (DE) are covered.
